\(A: Strain = New\),

\(B: Test = Positive\),

\(C: Test= false\  Positive\  for\  the\  previous\),

\(P(A) = 0.01\),

\(P(B)=0.99\),

\(P(C)=0.02\),

\(P(B)=P(B)\cdot P(A)+P(B)\cdot P(C)\),

\[\begin{aligned}P(A|B)&=\frac{P(B|A)\cdot P(B)}{P(B)}\\&=\frac{0.01\cdot 0.99}{0.99\cdot 0.01+0.02\cdot 0.99}\\&=\frac{1}{3}\end{aligned}\].

    \hypertarget{question-2-the-new-normal}{%
\section{Question 2 The new Normal}\label{question-2-the-new-normal}}

Recall that the normal distribution \(N(\mu, \delta ^2)\)

\hypertarget{a}{%
\subsection{a)}\label{a}}

\(\because X\backsim N(0,1)\),

than, we know that

\[\begin{aligned}E(Y) &= E(aX+b)\\&=aE(X)+b\\&=b\end{aligned}\]

and

\[\begin{aligned}Var(Y)&=Var(aE(X)+b)\\&=a^2Var(X)\\&=a^2\end{aligned}\].

\hypertarget{b}{%
\subsection{b)}\label{b}}

Let \(x\) be any real number. We will first compute
\(F_Y(y)=P(Y\leq x)\). Since \(Y=aX+b\), we know that

\[\begin{aligned}F(y) &= P(Y\leq y) \\&= P(zX+b\leq y) \\&= P(X\leq \frac{y-b}{a}) \\&= \Phi (\frac{y-b}{b})\end{aligned}\]

So \(F_Y(y)=\Phi(\frac{y-b}{a})\).

Differentiating this with respect to \(y\), we find

\[\begin{aligned}f_Y(y) &= \frac{1}{a}\Phi(\frac{y-b}{a})\\&=\frac{1}{a}\phi(\frac{y-b}{a})\\&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}a}exp{-(\frac{(y-b)^2}{2a^2})}\end{aligned}\].

\hypertarget{c}{%
\subsection{c)}\label{c}}

from \(b)\), we knows that, \(f_Y(y)\) is \(pdf\) of \(N(b,a^2)\)
distribution.

\hypertarget{d}{%
\subsection{d)}\label{d}}

\(\because\) \(E(Y)=b\) and \(Var(Y)=a^2\),

than, we know that

\[\begin{aligned}E(Z)&=E(cY+d)\\&=cE(Y)+d\\&=cb+d\end{aligned}\]

and

\[\begin{aligned}Var(Z)&=Var(cY+d)\\&=c^2Var(Y)\\&=c^2a^2\end{aligned}\]

\(\therefore\) \(Z\backsim N(cb+d, c^2a^2)\).
